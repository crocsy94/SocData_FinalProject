When does Melbourne go to work?

We investigated data from parking sensors and pedestrian counters on the streets to figure out when does the city wake up and go to work. To see our results, play around with the visualizations below.

An average day...
Is it different on holidays?

To figure out this question, we widened our scope for the year of 2016. The parking dataset contains information about which parking bay was occupied in what time period. We analysed the frequency of people leaving parking places for each day. The chart below displays the average number of parking bay usage for each day as well as the average number of citizens counted by a sensor in downtown. The two dataset correlates well with each other, the numbers are quite low during the weekends for both the parking and pedestrian data. It should be noted that the sensors are mainly located in the Central Business District of Melbourne, where the number of households are barely mentionable and only offices and shopping centers can be found. Some odd patterns can be noticed at the beginning of the year, somewhere around April and at the end of the year. Click on the chart to gain further insight about these periods.
